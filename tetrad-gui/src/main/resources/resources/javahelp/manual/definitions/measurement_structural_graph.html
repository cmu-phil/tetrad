Definition: Measurement/structural model

A specific graphical model where all observed variables are indicators of some latent predictor (i.e., each observed modelNode has at least one latent parent), and no observed predictor is a cause of any latent predictor (but they may be causes of another observed variables). The model describing which latents are causes of which observed variables, and how the observed variables are related, is called the measurement model. The model describing how latents are causally related is called the structural model.
